01.

project overview

The Hubble Space Telescope (HST) has been a beacon of discovery in astronomy since its launch in 1990. However, its website has fallen behind in delivering an engaging and user-friendly experience to a diverse and growing audience of space enthusiasts, researchers, and the general public. This project aims to revamp the Hubble Space Telescope website with a focus on enhancing its user interface (UI) to provide a more captivating, informative, and responsive online hub....

This project was started in order to participate in Creative Minds 2023 competitionorganized by Arena Animation and Aptech. This project was sent as a participating entry under Mobile Website Design category. I took it up as a practice project for my portfolio and did UI research ( which you'll see in the later parts of this case study ) to better the website's experience. Results to this competition are still awaiting. Wish me luck and read ahead!

05.

design solutions

before

Image carousel with indicators on the far sides and no quick information is provided. Auto flow is not there which requires extra clicking by the user.

after

Circular auto image carousel with changing subtitles are given to provide user with quick information. Indicators to control the flow and a View All CTA to explore all images.

before

Visual design of announcements section does not match the style and aesthetic of the entire website. Text and links are placed too close to each other which creates clutter on the page.

after

The focus is on one specific card at a time, helps the user to interact and read information properly. Not providing the user with a lot of information also makes the design accessible.

before

Hero banner for 30th Anniversary of Hubble Telescope is does not give off a very celebration aura. The 30 years logo is also not properly visible which should be the highlight of this web page.

after

Keeping the viewport height of the banner to be 100% and placing the 30 years logo as well the Hubble Telescope at the center of the screen highlights the true purpose and aesthetic of this web page.

before

Images page has 3 filters after which the user can get desired image gallery. To look for more information, user clicks on an image and is redirected to another page, increasing unnecessary user clicks.

after

Auto carousel with a futuristic feel which matches the websites aesthetic. Category tabs are given above for easy filtering. Image information is presented in an overlay instead of a new page.

06.

what i learned ?

Modern Design Trends

I gained insights into current web design trends and how they can be applied to create a visually appealing and up-to-date website. I discovered how interactive components like videos, carousels and overlays can make complex topics more accessible.

Responsive Web Design

I understood the necessity of responsive web design to ensure that the website looks and functions well on various devices and screen sizes.

Community Building

I learned the importance of community building features such as forums and social media integration in fostering a sense of belonging and encouraging discussions among users.

07.

next steps

The next steps in the project will involve improvements in usability, design aesthetics, and overall functionality. Accessibility audit can be conducted to ensure inclusivity for all users, and a focus on mobile responsiveness will optimize the sites performance across various devices.

Implementation of interactive components like 3D models of telescope and video content for studies conducted, will further enrich the educational aspects of the website.
A sense of community among space enthusiasts will be intensified through forums and social media integrations.
